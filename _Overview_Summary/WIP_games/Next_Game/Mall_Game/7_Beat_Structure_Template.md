# The 7-Beat Game Structure Template

*A reusable framework for location-based puzzle narrative games with AI guide characters*

---

## Overview

This structure creates a 90-minute Hero's Journey where players solve 5 distinct puzzles (Beats 2-6) framed by a problem (Beat 1) and a celebration (Beat 7).

**The Simple Structure:**
- **Beat 1**: Problem revealed (no puzzle)
- **Beats 2-6**: Five puzzle types (one per beat)
- **Beat 7**: Resolution & celebration (no puzzle)

---

## BEAT 1: THE PROBLEM
**Duration**: 5-10 minutes  
**Location**: Starting point

### Narrative Purpose
- **THE PROBLEM IS REVEALED**: Something is wrong/missing/broken
- Stakes established immediately
- Guide character introduced (worried/urgent)
- Call to action: "Help me solve this!"
- No puzzle yet - just understanding the situation

### Emotional State
- **Players**: Surprised, concerned, wanting to help
- **Guide Character**: Worried, urgent, needs their help

### Mechanics
- **NO PUZZLE** - This is pure story
- App-based: Video or message reveals the problem
- Players understand what's at stake
- Ready to start puzzle-solving

### Key Questions to Answer
- What's wrong? What's missing?
- Who needs help and why?
- What are the stakes if players fail?
- Who is the guide character?
- What's the emotional hook?

### Lost Verse Example
- Maya's panicked video: "Lex is missing!"
- Song's final verse is gone
- Town is getting aggressive without the complete song
- Vale might be involved
- Players agree to help find the missing lyrics

### Mall Game Application
- What video/message reveals the problem?
- Who is the guide character?
- What's missing/wrong in the mall?
- Why should players care?
- What happens if they don't help?

## BEAT 2: PUZZLE TYPE 1
**Duration**: 15-20 minutes  
**Location**: First puzzle location(s)

### Narrative Purpose
- First real challenge
- Players prove they can help
- Often involves MOVEMENT or COLLECTION
- Sets pattern: "We solve puzzles → We get information"
- First piece of solution acquired

### Emotional State
- **Players**: Determined, focused, working together
- **Guide Character**: Encouraging, excited when they succeed

### Mechanics
- **PUZZLE TYPE 1**: Often repetitive/collecting
- Pattern that repeats (once you get it, you do it multiple times)
- Rewards observation and pattern recognition
- Results in first key piece of information

### Common Puzzle Types for Beat 2
- Letter extraction from multiple locations/signs
- Number collecting from series of objects
- Photo-based pattern recognition
- Scavenger hunt with clues
- Code decryption (simple)
- **Key trait**: Repetition builds investment through TIME spent

### Key Questions to Answer
- What's the first puzzle type?
- Where do players go?
- What pattern do they repeat?
- How many times?
- What do they collect/discover?
- What information does this unlock?

### Lost Verse Example
- Number patterns extract letters from memorial squares
- Pattern: "5-3" = 5th word, 3rd letter
- Visit 8 different square locations
- Collect letters spelling: "AND WHEN THE SHADOWS CLEAR"
- First piece of missing lyrics acquired

### Mall Game Application
- What's Puzzle Type 1?
- Where in the mall does it happen?
- What mall features/signs does it use?
- What do players collect?
- What does this reveal?

---

## BEAT 3: PUZZLE TYPE 2
**Duration**: 10-15 minutes  
**Location**: Primary puzzle location

### Narrative Purpose
- Second puzzle builds on first
- New information revealed
- Often involves OBSERVATION skills
- Video or clue provides the key
- Second piece of solution acquired

### Emotional State
- **Players**: Confident, getting into rhythm
- **Guide Character**: Celebrating progress, providing next clue

### Mechanics
- **PUZZLE TYPE 2**: Moderate difficulty
- Often involves finding patterns in video or environment
- Rewards careful observation
- May involve codes, sequences, or matching

### Common Puzzle Types for Beat 3
- Video analysis (spot clues in footage)
- Code extraction from visual elements
- Sequence puzzles
- Matching puzzles
- Number/letter pattern recognition
- **Key trait**: Observation is key

### Key Questions to Answer
- What's the second puzzle type?
- Is there a video with clues?
- What must players observe carefully?
- What code/pattern do they extract?
- What information does this reveal?

### Lost Verse Example
- Video shows Lex touching three specific lampposts
- Players find those lampposts in Dana Park
- Each has a yellow numbered sticker
- Numbers in order = code 593
- Unlocks more lyrics + word search puzzle

### Mall Game Application
- What's Puzzle Type 2?
- Is there a video? What's in it?
- What mall features appear?
- What code/pattern emerges?
- What does this unlock?

---

## BEAT 4: PUZZLE TYPE 3
**Duration**: 15-20 minutes  
**Location**: Same or nearby location

### Narrative Purpose
- Mid-game complexity increase
- Requires integration of environment
- Often uses physical location features
- Something suspicious/concerning emerges in story
- Third piece of solution acquired

### Emotional State
- **Players**: Deep in the zone, problem-solving mode
- **Guide Character**: Increasingly worried about antagonist

### Mechanics
- **PUZZLE TYPE 3**: More complex, multi-step
- Often integrates location features
- May require finding things in environment
- Moderate-to-high difficulty

### Common Puzzle Types for Beat 4
- Word search (with external word list from location)
- Environmental scavenger hunt
- Cipher/decoding with key from location
- Multi-step logic puzzles
- Math or calculation puzzles
- **Key trait**: Uses location as puzzle key

### Key Questions to Answer
- What's the third puzzle type?
- What location features does it use?
- How do players find the "key" to solving it?
- What concerning information emerges in story?
- What does this reveal about antagonist?

### Lost Verse Example
- Word search puzzle (no word list provided)
- Must find first/last words on 8 quote signs in park
- Search for those words in grid
- Remaining letters = more lyrics
- Vale's suspicious behavior revealed in video

### Mall Game Application
- What's Puzzle Type 3?
- What mall features does it integrate?
- How do players find what they need?
- What story revelation happens?
- What's suspicious/concerning?

## BEAT 5: PUZZLE TYPE 4
**Duration**: 15-20 minutes  
**Location**: Same location

### Narrative Purpose
- High complexity puzzle
- May involve multiple steps
- Tension building: antagonist motives clearer
- Often requires info from previous puzzles
- Fourth piece of solution acquired

### Emotional State
- **Players**: Challenged but determined, sense of urgency building
- **Guide Character**: Urgent, suspicious, time pressure felt

### Mechanics
- **PUZZLE TYPE 4**: High complexity
- May combine multiple steps or concepts
- Often spatial or visual reasoning
- Requires sustained focus

### Common Puzzle Types for Beat 5
- Maze puzzles (path-finding)
- Spatial reasoning puzzles
- Multi-step codes
- Layered puzzles (solve A to unlock B)
- Logic grid puzzles
- **Key trait**: Highest single-puzzle difficulty

### Key Questions to Answer
- What's the fourth puzzle type?
- How does it connect to earlier clues?
- What makes it challenging?
- What story tension peaks here?
- How is antagonist revealed/confirmed?

### Lost Verse Example
- Maze puzzle: follow path, collect letters = lyrics
- Those lyrics open another safe
- Video shows Vale photographing Lex's work
- Lex mutters about "colors at A, B, C"
- Vale's villain status nearly confirmed

### Mall Game Application
- What's Puzzle Type 4?
- How complex is it?
- What earlier info does it use?
- What's the story climax?
- How is antagonist confirmed?

---

## BEAT 6: PUZZLE TYPE 5
**Duration**: 15-20 minutes  
**Location**: Same location

### Narrative Purpose
- Final puzzle before resolution
- Often integrates multiple previous elements
- Highest tension: stakes clear, antagonist confirmed
- All information collected
- Final piece of solution acquired

### Emotional State
- **Players**: Intense focus, racing to finish, determined
- **Guide Character**: Most urgent, worried, race against time

### Mechanics
- **PUZZLE TYPE 5**: Most integrated puzzle
- May require info from multiple earlier puzzles
- Combines observation + logic + environment
- Final unlock before revelation

### Common Puzzle Types for Beat 6
- Sudoku or logic grid (with environmental key)
- Color/pattern matching with location
- Multi-puzzle integration
- Complex codes requiring multiple inputs
- Final decryption puzzle
- **Key trait**: Integration of everything learned

### Key Questions to Answer
- What's the fifth puzzle type?
- How does it integrate earlier puzzles?
- What environmental elements does it use?
- What's the final piece of information?
- How is this the "last step" before resolution?

### Lost Verse Example
- Sudoku puzzle with colored squares
- Find colors in park environment:
  - Copper/brown = frog sculptures
  - Yellow = lamppost stickers
  - Red = emergency button
- Numbers in those colored squares = final code 286
- Unlocks final lyrics + complete song
- All pieces assembled

### Mall Game Application
- What's Puzzle Type 5?
- What does it integrate from earlier?
- What mall features are the key?
- What's the final information revealed?
- How does this set up the resolution?

## BEAT 7: RESOLUTION & CELEBRATION
**Duration**: 10-15 minutes  
**Location**: Celebration space (food court, specific store, etc.)

### Narrative Purpose
- **Understanding the deeper meaning**
- Final revelation/aha moment (often hidden message)
- Action required: players must DO something
- Problem resolved
- Celebration/reward
- Victory!

### Emotional State
- **Players**: Triumphant, proud, celebratory
- **Guide Character**: Relieved, grateful, celebrating with them

### Mechanics
- **NO NEW PUZZLE** - This is revelation + action
- Often: discover hidden message in complete solution
- Players must take physical action (sing, go somewhere, meet someone)
- Physical reward delivered

### Common Beat 7 Elements
- Acrostic message in solution
- Hidden meaning in completed puzzle
- Symbolic final code
- Physical action required (singing, declaring, moving)
- Meeting the guide character or rescued person
- Food/treat reward
- **Key trait**: Meaning matters more than mechanics

### Key Questions to Answer
- What's the hidden meaning/message?
- What revelation makes everything click?
- What action must players take?
- Where do they go for celebration?
- What's the physical reward?
- How does the story resolve?
- What did players accomplish (beyond puzzles)?

### Lost Verse Example
- Complete lyrics reveal acrostic message:
  - "YOUR VOICE MATTERS LISTEN TOGETHER AND UNDERSTAND"
- Realize: without final verse, song makes people demanding
- With final verse: song creates understanding
- Vale wanted incomplete version to control crowds
- Players must sing complete song together
- Magic restores town's understanding
- Meet at liquor store for Ben & Jerry's ice cream
- Heroes save the day!

### Mall Game Application
- What's the final revelation?
- What hidden meaning is revealed?
- What must players do?
- Where's the celebration?
- What's the reward?
- How does guide character celebrate with them?
- What's the deeper meaning of their victory?

## Quick Reference Chart

| Beat | Purpose | Mechanic | Duration |
|------|---------|----------|----------|
| 1 | Problem revealed | NO PUZZLE (story only) | 5-10 min |
| 2 | First challenge | PUZZLE 1 (repetitive/collection) | 15-20 min |
| 3 | Build momentum | PUZZLE 2 (observation) | 10-15 min |
| 4 | Increase complexity | PUZZLE 3 (environmental) | 15-20 min |

## Emotional & Story Arc

### Player Emotional Journey
- **Beat 1**: Concerned → "Someone needs help!"
- **Beat 2**: Determined → "We can do this!"
- **Beat 3**: Confident → "We're good at this!"
- **Beat 4**: Challenged → "This is getting serious..."
- **Beat 5**: Urgent → "We need to hurry!"
- **Beat 6**: Intense → "Final push!"
- **Beat 7**: Triumphant → "We did it!"

### Guide Character Arc
- **Beat 1**: Worried/desperate
- **Beat 2**: Encouraging/hopeful
- **Beat 3**: Excited about progress
- **Beat 4**: Suspicious/concerned
- **Beat 5**: Urgent/tense
- **Beat 6**: Most worried/time pressure
- **Beat 7**: Relieved/grateful/celebrating

### Story Information Flow
- **Beat 1**: Problem + stakes established
- **Beat 2**: First clue/info collected
- **Beat 3**: Backstory revealed (often via video)
- **Beat 4**: Antagonist suspected
- **Beat 5**: Antagonist motives clear
- **Beat 6**: All pieces assembled
- **Beat 7**: Deeper meaning revealed

## The 5 Puzzle Types (Beats 2-6)

### Selecting Your 5 Puzzles

**Puzzle 1 (Beat 2)**: Longest, repetitive, builds investment through time
- Letter extraction, number collection, pattern recognition
- 15-20 minutes of repeated pattern

**Puzzle 2 (Beat 3)**: Observation-based, moderate difficulty
- Video clues, code extraction, sequence puzzles
- 10-15 minutes of careful observation

**Puzzle 3 (Beat 4)**: Environmental integration, multi-step
- Word search, cipher, location-based puzzles
- 15-20 minutes using location features

**Puzzle 4 (Beat 5)**: High difficulty, logic/spatial reasoning
- Maze, logic grid, complex decoding
- 15-20 minutes of focused problem-solving

**Puzzle 5 (Beat 6)**: Integration puzzle, uses previous info
- Sudoku with environmental key, color-matching, multi-puzzle synthesis
- 15-20 minutes bringing everything together

## Critical Success Factors

✅ **Beat 1 hooks emotionally** - Players must care about the problem immediately

✅ **Puzzle progression feels natural** - Each puzzle type is distinct and builds on previous

✅ **Beat 2 is longest** - Repetition = time investment = emotional investment

✅ **Story escalates** - Tension increases from Beat 1 → Beat 6

✅ **Beat 7 reveals meaning** - The "why" behind everything becomes clear

✅ **Celebration is physical** - Real reward in real world (food, meeting, gift)

✅ **Guide character is believable** - Their emotional arc feels genuine via app

✅ **No busy work** - Every puzzle serves story and progression

---

*Use this template for any location-based app-driven puzzle game with 5 puzzle types wrapped in a story. The structure is simple: Problem → 5 Puzzles → Resolution.*

## Design Principles

### Mechanic Progression
- **Beat 2**: Simple (find it)
- **Beat 3**: Repetitive (pattern you repeat)
- **Beat 4**: Moderate (solve to unlock)
- **Beat 5**: Complex (multi-step)
- **Beat 6**: Most complex (integrated)
- **Beat 7**: Revelation (understand meaning)

### Emotional Arc
- **Start**: Casual → Concerned
- **Middle**: Determined → Confident → Urgent
- **End**: Triumphant → Celebratory

### Information Flow
- **Beat 2**: Problem revealed
- **Beat 3**: First clues collected
- **Beat 4**: First unlock + backstory
- **Beat 5**: Complexity + suspicion
- **Beat 6**: Tension + antagonist confirmed
- **Beat 7**: Truth + meaning revealed

### Video/Guide Placement
- **Beat 2**: Initial problem video
- **Beat 4**: First discovery video
- **Beat 5**: Concerning video
- **Beat 6**: Final clue video + villain reveal
- **Beat 7**: Resolution video + celebration

## Critical Success Factors

**Progressive Difficulty**: Each beat should be slightly harder than the last (except Beat 7 which is revelation, not difficulty)

**Emotional Investment**: Story beats must make players CARE about the outcome

**Physical Movement**: Ideally, players move through space (Beat 3) or explore a location deeply (Beats 4-6)

**Clear Stakes**: By Beat 5, players should understand exactly what's at risk

**Satisfying Resolution**: Beat 7 must feel earned and meaningful, not arbitrary

**Celebration**: Physical reward (food, meet character, gift) grounds the victory
